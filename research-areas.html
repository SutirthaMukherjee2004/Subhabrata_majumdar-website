Research Areas

Major themes pursued in the group, with representative papers and publication figures.

+3

Galaxy Cluster Cosmology

Read more

Clusters are used as precision cosmological probes through mass calibration, redshift distributions, scaling relations, and combinations of X-ray, SZ and optical measurements.

Relevant papers
  1. Deriving accurate galaxy cluster masses using X-ray thermodynamic profiles and graph neural networks
  2. Self calibration in cluster studies of dark energy
  3. Importance of cluster structural evolution in galaxy cluster surveys
+3

Sunyaev-Zel'dovich & Secondary CMB

Read more

The work follows CMB secondary anisotropies, thermal and non-thermal SZ signals, quasar bubbles, radio lobes, and their use as tracers of cluster and feedback physics.

Relevant papers
  1. Revisiting secondary CMB distortions due to kinetic SZ effect
  2. The non-thermal secondary CMB anisotropies from radio galaxy lobes
  3. Secondary anisotropies of the CMB
+3

Intracluster Medium & AGN Feedback

Read more

This theme studies how buoyant bubbles, sound waves, entropy injection, radio luminosity and feedback energy shape the thermodynamic history of the intracluster medium.

Relevant papers
  1. Heating of the intracluster medium by buoyant bubbles and sound waves
  2. Correlations of the feedback energy and BCG radio luminosity
  3. Excess entropy and energy feedback from cluster cores
+3

Circumgalactic Baryons

Read more

Cross-correlations of galaxy distributions with SZ and X-ray signals are used to probe diffuse baryons, gas fraction, pressure profiles and feedback around galaxies.

Relevant papers
  1. X-ray and SZ constraints on the properties of hot CGM
  2. Probing the circumgalactic baryons through cross-correlations
  3. CMB distortion from circumgalactic gas

Dark Matter, Curvature & Growth

Read more

The group also works on halo inference, dark matter phase-space distributions, cosmic curvature, dark energy constraints and large-scale homogeneity tests.

Relevant papers
  1. Beyond best-fits and model selection in cusp-core inference
  2. Constraints on cosmic curvature from chronometer and quasar observations
  3. Dark matter phase-space distribution and direct detection experiments

Radio Surveys & Cosmic Web

Read more

Low-frequency radio surveys, uGMRT, SKA-era observations and multi-wavelength survey forecasts are used to follow diffuse emission and large-scale structure.

Relevant papers
  1. Exploring diffuse radio emission in galaxy clusters and groups with uGMRT and SKA
  2. AGN feedback with the Square Kilometre Array
  3. Constraining the X-ray AGN halo occupation distribution

High-Redshift Cluster Surveys

Read more

Infrared and optical cluster surveys trace massive structures at redshifts above one, testing cluster selection, red-sequence methods and early structure growth.

Relevant papers
  1. SpARCS-South cluster survey at z = 1.34
  2. SpARCS-North clusters at z ~ 1.2
  3. Spitzer Adaptation of the Red-Sequence Cluster Survey

Dark Energy Forecasts & Systematics

Read more

Forecasts combine supernovae, clusters, CMB priors and survey observables to quantify dark energy constraints and sensitivity to systematics.

Relevant papers
  1. Post-Planck Dark Energy Constraints
  2. Cosmology with the largest galaxy cluster surveys
  3. Searching for systematics with the cosmic duality relation

Cosmic Homogeneity & Curvature Tests

Read more

Large-scale homogeneity, curvature and evolving dark energy models are tested with multiple tracers, chronometers, quasars and statefinder diagnostics.

Relevant papers
  1. The many scales to cosmic homogeneity
  2. Constraints on cosmic curvature
  3. Constraining Thawing Quintessence

Galactic Dark Matter & Dynamics

Galaxy rotation data, dwarf-spheroidal dynamics and phase-space inference connect astrophysical observations to direct-detection expectations.
